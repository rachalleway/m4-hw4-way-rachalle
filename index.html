Lana Del Rey

About

Elizabeth Grant, better known by her stage name, Lana Del Rey, was born June 21, 1985 and is an American singer and songwriter. Her music is known for its unique cinematic sound and occurring themes of tragedy, romance, glamor, melancholia, and 1950s-1960s Americana culture and imagery. She has been accredited as being "one of the most influential singer-songwriters of the 21st century." She was raised in upstate New York and has spoke about her feelings of existential dread and alienation that she felt at an early age. According to Grant, "I had a sort of a philosophical crisis. I couldn't believe that we were mortal. For some reason that knowledge sort of overshadowed my experience. I was unhappy for some time. I got into a lot of trouble. I used to drink a lot. That was a hard time in my life."

In her late teens, Grant became interested in songwriting and would perform in New York City nightclubs. Though she would sing and perform frequently, she never planned on pursuing a career in music seriously. While playing in clubs in Brooklyn, she began developing a devoted fan base in the underground scene, but states, "we were playing for each other at that pointâ€”and that was it."

Career

For her debut album, Grant chose the stage name "Lana Del Rey" as it reminded her of glamor and at the time, she was spending a lot of her time in Miami. The name was also a combination of 1940s film actress, Lana Turner and the Ford Del Rey Sedan, which was produced in Brazil from 1981-1991.

Her self-titled debut album under the stage name was released in early 2010. However, the album was withdrawn 3 months later after Del Rey bought the rights to her album back from her recording company and ended her contract with them as she felt like "nothing was happening."

Born to Die

In 2011, Del Rey uploaded self-made videos for her songs, "Video Games" and "Blue Jeans" to YouTube with the former going viral on the platform, making her an internet sensation. It would be the debut single of her new album, Born to Die, under Stranger Records and released in early 2012. Born to Die debuted at number 2 in the US Billboard 200 and was the fifth best-selling album of 2012. The deluze edition, Born to Die: The Paradise Edition was even norminated for Best Pop Vocal Album at the 56th Annual Grammy Awards.

Ultraviolence

Ultraviolence was released in mid-2014 and debuted number one in 12 countries including the US. Singles included, "West Coast," "Shade of Cool," "Ultraviolence," and "Brooklyn Baby." Critics characterized the record as sounding darker, more psychedelic, and desert-rock influenced.

Honeymoon and Lust for Life

Honeymoon, Del Rey's fourth album, was released in mid-late 2015 and was also met with critical acclaim. In November 2015, she won the Trailblazer Award at Billboard's Women in Music Ceremony.

Her fifth studio album, Lust for Life, was released in mid-2017 and received generally favorable reviews. It was nominated for Best Pop Vocal Album at the 60th Grammy Awards.

Norman F*cking Rockwell!

Her sixth studio album, Norman F*cking Rockwell! was released in mid-2019 and received widespread critical acclaim. It is the best-reviewed album in her career to date and was deemed an instant "pop classic." The album received two Grammy nominations, one for Album of the Year and the other for Song of the Year for the self-titled track.

Chemtrails Over the Country Club and Blue Bannisters

In 2021, Del Rey released two albums, Chemtrails Over the Country Club and Blue Bannisters, respectively. The former was met with some critical acclaim. However, with some personal controversies regarding her views on feminism and behavior during the COVID-19 pandemic, the albums were not met with as much buzz as her previous releases.

Influences

Musical Style

Del Rey's music has been described as "Hollywood sadcore" by some and can be uniquely identified for its cinematic sound and references to pop culture. Strong themes of Americana and American nostalgia has has many classify her as alternative pop, rock, indie pop, and psychedelic pop. With some of her earlier albums, there are incorporated sonical elements of trap music, lo-fi, and hip-hop.

She accredits her unique sound to a wide, almost random, assortment of musical influences like Andrew Lloyd Weber, Frank Sinatra, Billy Holiday, Miles Davis, Nirvana, Bob Dylan, Elvis Presley, Bruce Springsteen, Britney Spears, Cat Power, Eminem, and Amy Winehouse to name a few. She also accredits film director, David Lynch and painter, Pablo Picasso to be some of her influences.

Impact

Billboard accredits Born to Die as being one of the main catalysts for the shift in pop music in the mid-2010s from EDM to a moodier, hip-hop sound. Del Rey's sound has influenced the sound of other alternative and pop artists like Taylor Swift, Halsey, Selena Gomez, Billie Eilish, Sia, and Sky Ferreira. The Washington Post listed Del Rey as the only musician on their "Decade of Influence" list.
